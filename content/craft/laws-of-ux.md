# Laws of UX

Named cognitive limits → one composition directive each. Covers *what to compose*,
not *how to render*.

- **Hick's Law / Choice Overload** — keep a decision to 3–5 options. Pricing: 3–4 tiers with exactly **one** marked "recommended".
- **Miller's Law** — chunk into ≤5 groups; don't present 12 ungrouped fields or nav items.
- **Von Restorff (isolation)** — make the one thing you want clicked visually distinct — and back the distinction with more than color (size, position, a label).
- **Fitts's Law** — primary actions are large and close to where attention already is; don't hide the main CTA in a far corner.
- **Jakob's Law** — match platform conventions (where nav, search, close, and submit live) unless you have a strong reason.
- **Goal-Gradient / Zeigarnik** — show progress on multi-step flows; people push harder near a visible finish.
- **Peak-End** — invest the most craft in the decisive moment and the final confirmation.
