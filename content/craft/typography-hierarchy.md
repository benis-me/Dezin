# Typography hierarchy

Hierarchy is **contrast**, not a list of sizes. A reader should know where to look
first, second, third — without reading a word.

## Contract

- **One dominant entry point per screen** (the largest, heaviest, or most-spaced element). Exactly one.
- Information is recoverable by skimming: titles → subheads → body → meta.
- Rhythm is intentional: vary section spacing to group related content; equal spacing everywhere reads as a wireframe.

## The five hierarchy vectors

scale · weight · spacing (whitespace around) · tracking · alignment. The dominant
element should win on **at least two** vectors, not size alone.

## Anti-patterns

- A graduated weight ladder (every level a different weight) — pick weight contrast at the top, hold it elsewhere.
- Size-only hierarchy (everything the same weight/color, just bigger) — flat and dull.
- Uniform section spacing — no grouping signal.
